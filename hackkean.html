FAQ

  • What is a hackathon?

    • Hackathons are like marathons for coders. You get to build a project in a short amount of time. There are usually other things found within the hackathon such as workshops, games, networking opportunities, etc.

  • What do we do in HackKean?

    • You get to build a tech project of your choice. You are encouraged to be as creative as possible. Build a website for a made-up company that sells jetpacks, a video game where you play as a magical space ninja, or an AI that can control climate change. What you do is completely up to you.

  • How many teammates can I have?

    • You can make a team of up-to 3 students.

  • What if I can't finish my project on time?

    • That is fine, submit what you have. We want to include people from every skill level, and we understand that there might not be enough time to finish every project. Submitting something is better than nothing. Maybe you have a really cool idea? Maybe you were only missing minor details?

  • Is the event in person?

    • Due to concerns regarding Covid-19, we will hold the event remotely through Zoom and Slack.

  • How do I submit my project?

    • We will give you submission details on the day of the event.

  • What do I get from winning?

    • Firstly, you get the honor and bragging rights of being a hackathon winner. Secondly, you get a monetary prize distributed among your team members.

Guidelines

Regestration Guidelines

  1. Participants must be students currently pursuing k-12 education, a graduate degree, or an undergraduate degree.
  2. Participants may register as a team of no more than 3 individuals. Prizes are to be shared among the individuals.
  3. Registration must be complete before the registration deadline on February 12th, unless stated otherwise by the Kean ACM team.

Participation Guidelines

  1. Work must be done by the participants. If any outside sources are used (excluding public libraries or APIs), they are to be documented. You may use code snippets found online if the source is documented. Using code without including its source may result in your disqualification. Copying an entire project or most of a project may also result in disqualification.
  2. Be respectful to other participants. Bullying and harassment of other participants will not be tolerated and will result in immediate disqualification
  3. Recording of the event or any workshop is not allowed. Recordings may be provided later by the ACM, but participants are not to record the event or any workshop within the event.
  4. Do not share internal links and information without permission.
  5. Violating any of the above guidelines may result in disqualification. The decision to disqualify participants is ultimately up to Kean University and the Kean ACM team.
